Food Security

According to the United Nations' (UN) Committee on World Food Security, food security is defined as people, "at all times", having "Physical, Social, and Economic" access to "sufficient, safe and nutritious food" that meets their "dietary needs for an active and healthy life." (United Nations, 1996)

Project

In line with the definition of Food security provided by UN, we have decided to find out how

  • Physical Factors
  • Social Factors
  • Economic Factors
have affected the dietary requirements for an active and healthy lifestyle of the population of each country.

Physical Factors

We define Physical Factors as individuals having a small portion of control over these factors; of which we have two factors.

  • Percentage of population having eating disorders for each country in percent

    There are four types of eating disorders, of which some results in overconsumption while others lead to underconsumption of food. (AWARESG, 2022) However, our main concern for eating disorders is that individuals have a mental "insecurity" with food which results in a unhealthy lifestyle.

  • Land used for agriculture for each country in km2 per capita

    The greater the agricultural land per capita suggests that there is more allocated land to help grow crops per person. Assuming production/growing of food per land per capita is constant, it is safe to say that the greater land per capita, the greater the "access to sufficient ... food".

Social Factors

We define Social Factors as individuals not being able to control these factors; of which we have 2 factors.

  • Percentage of population having access to basic drinking water services in percent

    Water is an essential to food security. (United Nations) Water is used in many ways to ensure a food security; to grow crops, to clean food, and to even cook the food. Hence, we have also considered how the access to basic drink water relates to the "access to sufficient, safe and nutritious food".

  • Carbon Dioxide (CO2) emitted for each country per agricultural land area in Million Metric Tons(MMT)/km2

    CO2 helps plant grow through photosynthesis which helps increase crop yields. It might seem contradictory to choose this factor, however, too much CO2 also comes at the "cost of lower nutritional quality as plants accumulate more carbohydrates and less minerals" (L ebi, 2019). The increase in carbhoydrates result in greater calorie yield which fits our model since we are focusing on calories per capita per day.

Economic Factors

We define Economic Factors as factors that is due to the state of the market around the world, uncontrollable by individuals.

  • Percentage of population employed in agriculture, forestry and fishing in percent

    We believe the greater the percentage of population allocated to agriculture, the faster and more efficient the growing of crops are. This allows greater harvest and higher "access to ... food" in general.

  • Gross Domestic Product per capita, adjusted for PPP US$ per capita

    Increasing GDP per capita can increase purchasing power of individuals. It also ensures that food prices are affordable allowing greater Food Security. (K. Singh, 2020). Further research also shows that "inflation and population growth rates are the most responsible for food insecurity in most developing countries" (Fernandes. M., 2022). Hence, for the simplicity of our model and to cover economics factors, we will focus on GDP per capita and how it affects food security in general.

Dependent Value

Ratio of average calories provided in kcal per capita per day to average calorie requirements per capita per day in kcal per capita per day

To measure food security, more specifically the dietary needs for an active and healthy life, we will be calculating it through the use of the ratio of average calories provided per individual per day over the average calorie requirement per individual per day. If the ratio is less than 1, we will deem that there there is food insecurity as the calorie requirement has not been met and the dietary needs of each individual has not been met. However, if the ratio is more than 1, we will deem that there is sufficient food security as the calories requirement has been met and dietary needs of each individual has been met.
